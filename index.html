Upholding the rights of public servants of Seychelles

Who We Are

We are an independent appeal board established under article 145 of the constitution of Seychelles to adjudicate a limited scope of employment-related grievances brought forward by public service employees in Seychelles. We provide a fair and impartial platform for resolving disputes involving:

  • a) Appointments to public service positions
  • b) Promotions within the public service
  • c) Disciplinary actions taken against public officers
  • d) Termination of appointments
  • e) Decisions regarding the qualifications of applicants or serving officers

As an independent body, we are committed to upholding justice and ensuring the accountability of government decision-making that affects public servants.

Our Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To protect the rights of public servants by delivering accessible, impartial, and no-cost resolution services for grievances stemming from decisions made by the Government of Seychelles and its agencies.

Our Vision

To be a trusted authority in administrative justice—resolving public service grievances with integrity, fairness, and efficiency while promoting transparency and confidence in public administration.

What We Do

Our Services

If you are a public servant personally affected by a decision made by a government agency, we are here to help. All of our services are free and confidential. We provide:

  • Guidance and support for filing complaints related to dismissals, demotions, suspensions, or denial of benefits
  • Clarification of policies and regulations affecting your rights and employment status
  • Informal mediation to help resolve disputes between employees and public service employers
  • Assistance to rebuild workplace relationships and reduce conflict-related stress
  • Referrals to other resources if your case falls outside our mandate

Who Can Use Our Services?

  • Our services are available to public servants and government employees who have been personally impacted by employment-related decisions made by a government agency.
  • Not sure if you're eligible? Contact us—we’re here to help.
